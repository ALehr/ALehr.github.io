Books

Aristotle and Dante Discover the Secrets of the Universe

by Benjamin Alire Saenz

★★★★★

Easily one of my favorite books, along with the sequel. This was my third time reading it, and it holds up. I'm excited to discuss this one with my students at the start of term.

Aristotle and Dante Dive into the Waters of the World

by Benjamin Alire Saenz

★★★★★

I love this book as much as the first volume. This was my third time reading it, and it was just as moving as the first time I read it.

As the title implies, this second volume is about Aristotle and Dante figuring out where they fit in the broader world of adults and just how difficult that can be. The book explores racism, classism, and homophobia in a frank but age-appropriate manner. An essential read, in my opinion.

The only thing I don't love about this book is how overly earnest all the characters are. It feels a bit artificial and cringy to me, even for a YA book. That said, it's not a major distraction and does not detract from all the things I love about this book.

Darius the Great Is Not Okay

by Adib Khorram

★★★★☆

It's cute and it reads fast since it's YA. I loved the discussion of mental illness, especially among male characters. I also learned a lot about Iran and Persian culture.

My Brother's Husband

by Gengoroh Tagame

★★★★☆

I wasn't expecting to like this manga as much as I did, but I really enjoyed it. It's very cutesy and wholesome, even though it deals with some heavy themes.

The Perks of Being a Wallflower

by Stephen Chbosky

★★★★★

I haven't read this book since I was in high school, and I forgot how heavy it is. It doesn't shy away from the pain of being a lonely adolescent, and it talks frankly about all the activities that parents often don't want to think about their kids partaking in. It's a very honest, kind, and empathetic book, and I think it holds up.

Comics & Graphic Novels

The Magic Fish

by Trung Le Nguyen

★★★★★

Such a sweet comic. A beautiful little story about family, immigration, culture, language, and queerness. This one checks all my boxes.

Films

Barbarian

★★★★☆

Holy shit what an opening! The introductory sequence lasts almost 45 minutes, but it's phenomenal. Masterful suspense, and it really sets the tone for the film.

But also the decisions? That people make?? In this MOVIE??!!! Grade-A horror film idiocy.

And Justin Long's character is such an amazing douche bag that I was really craving his torture.

My only gripe is that the second half of the film wasn't as scary as the first. Still had fun watching it, though, and I'm excited for Zach Cregger's new movie, Weapons.

Beau Is Afraid

★★★☆☆

I'm glad I watched it, but I was honestly disappointed. I like Ari Aster's other films so much that I guess I had pretty high expectations for this one. It wasn't very scary, and TBH I was pretty confused about what was going on most of the time.

KPop Demon Hunters

★★★★☆

It's a silly kids' movie, but it's a lot of fun. Daniel and I have watched it a few times, both on our own and with my niece. He's also been listening to the soundtrack a lot, and I'm not tired of it yet, so that says something right there.

Sinners

★★★★☆

Visually stunning, phenomenal music. The plot was on the predictable side, but it was still a very fun movie.

Weapons

★★★★★

Holy shit! Such a good horror film! It's very well structured and maintains suspense throughout the runtime. The acting was great, the story was engaging, the minimal visuals were employed very well. Nothing bad to say about this one.

Series

The Boyfriend

★★★☆☆

Very cute and wholesome, just as all the reviews I've read promised. I don't usually watch reality TV, but I wanted to watch this one a) to support queer media, and b) everyone was saying how sweet it was. I don't know that I would recommend it, but I will probably watch a second season if they ever release one in the US.

Frieren

★★★★★

Fully binged it over the course of about a week, so that says something right there. It's fun, and it's touching. It's also packed full of great D&D references that you'll appreciate if you play, but they aren't essential to know what's going on or to enjoy the show.

I can't wait for season 2 to come out, and I'll probably read the manga in the meantime.

What We Do in the Shadows

★★★★★

Honestly one of the best TV commedies I've ever seen. So consistently good, and the series finale was very satisfying.
